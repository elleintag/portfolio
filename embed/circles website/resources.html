A Glimpse at Foster Care in Canada

Below are a number of resources focusing on youth in foster care in Canada, with a particular emphasis on the challenges faced by youth when they age out.

  • Hearing about the foster care system from those who have lived through it:
  • My Real Life Book: Report from the Youth Leaving Care Hearings


  • A general overview of some of the challenges and barriers that youth leaving foster care experience, along with some supports that currently exist
  • “Life After Foster Care in Canada” (Macleans, Nov. 19, 2019) (Macleans, Nov. 19, 2019)


  • A straightforward and easy to understand analysis of academic research and articles on outcomes for youth leaving foster care
  • Exploring Youth Outcomes After Aging Out of Care (developed by the woman highlighted in the above Macleans article)


  • Two reports that focus on the negative financial costs to former youth in care, as well as wider society, caused by the aging out process
  • “The High Costs of ‘Aging Out’ of Foster Care in Ontario” (TVO Today, Jan. 5, 2022) (TVO Today, Jan. 5, 2022)


  • A very in depth, pan Canadian study that explores the varied causes of youth homelessness, as well as providing some preventative strategies
  • Without a Home: The National Youth Homelessness Survey
  • “‘Half The Time I Felt Like Nobody Loved Me’: The Costs of ‘Aging Out’ of State Guardianship in Ontario>


  • A collection of articles on the affects of foster care on youth in Canada
  • A collection of articles on the connection between foster care and homelessness


  • This paper reviews the literature on foster care and explains the issues that foster children experience. It also addresses why the foster care system is failing youth, and gives practical suggestions for solutions.
  • The Foster Care Systems are Failing Foster Children
